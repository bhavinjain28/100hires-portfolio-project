## 2023: You're over-complicating cold email personalization

- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_youre-over-complicating-cold-email-personalization-activity-7095736943946203136-HgsA
- **Collected:** 2026-06-20

> You're over complicating cold email personalization. It's all about logic.
>
> What do you sell?
> What problem does that solve?
> What told you they have that problem?
>
> If you can answer those questions and be specific while using the words your prospect would use you've got everything you need to write a stellar cold email.
>
> - Why you thought they had problem
> - Problem you think they have
> - What you do to solve problem
> - Open it up for conversation
>
> voila. A cold email that's better than 96% of what sits in your buyer's inbox.

**Why collected:** Strips personalization down to three diagnostic questions. The "what told you they have that problem" question is the trigger logic that maps directly to Crawford's PQS framework and Braun's Trigger step, connecting three different experts' copy frameworks.

## 2023: Personalized cold emails get 1200% more replies

- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_personalized-cold-emails-get-1200-more-replies-activity-7087793349016911873-D6ZV
- **Collected:** 2026-06-20

> Personalized cold emails get 1200% more replies than automated ones.
>
> But "personalization" doesn't mean what most people think it means.
>
> The core question is: **Why am I interrupting you?** You need to answer that with the prospect's current situation — proof you understand what's happening in their world right now, not just their job title.
>
> Light research: one data point (software tool used) → craft a message
> Deep research: synthesize multiple details → create a distinctive, customized perspective
>
> Mass emails using token insertion feel like spam. Batch sending might generate meetings short-term but damages email deliverability for your entire organization. One-to-one personalized outreach yields dramatically higher reply rates without the collateral damage.

**Why collected:** Lavender's platform data backing the "why am I interrupting you" framing, the exact question that distinguishes relevant outreach from noise. Pairs with the deliverability point (batch damage to domain reputation) which connects the copy and infrastructure stages.

## 2023: The biggest cold email mistake around relevancy

- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_the-biggest-cold-email-mistake-i-see-folks-activity-7072920902941749248-FKif
- **Collected:** 2026-06-20

> The biggest cold email mistake I see folks making when they approach "relevancy" or "personalization": their research doesn't tie together.
>
> You can't front-load their LinkedIn headline and then pitch something unrelated to it. Every element — trigger, problem statement, social proof, CTA — has to pull in the same direction.
>
> Personalization isn't decoration. It's the logical chain that connects what you observed about them to why you're reaching out.

**Why collected:** Anti-pattern that directly applies to quality-control in the copy stage; "research doesn't tie together" is one of the most common failure modes in AI-generated outreach. Useful as a playbook checklist item.
